1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give website owners information about how their site is being used.

Cookies can be "first-party" (set by Darsync) or "third-party" (set by other services we use, such as Google Analytics). They can also be "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (stored until they expire or you delete them).

2. Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are strictly necessary for the website to function. They enable core features such as security, session management, and accessibility preferences. These cookies do not collect personal information for marketing purposes and cannot be disabled without affecting the functionality of the website.

3. Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our website. These cookies collect information about your visit, including pages viewed, time spent, and how you arrived at our site. This data is aggregated and anonymized. We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) which does not store IP addresses.

The information collected is used solely to improve our website content and user experience. We do not use analytics data to identify individual visitors.

4. Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies are used to track visitors across websites to allow the display of relevant advertisements and to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. We use Google Ads conversion tracking to understand which ads lead to actions on our site (such as form submissions or demo bookings).

These cookies are only set with your consent (where required by law) and can be managed through our cookie settings or your browser preferences.

6. How to Manage & Delete Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

6.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to manage cookies through their settings. You can typically find these options in the "Privacy" or "Security" section of your browser's preferences. Here are instructions for common browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies and site data

6.2 Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on, which is available for all major browsers.

6.3 Google Ads Personalization

You can manage your Google Ads preferences and opt out of personalized advertising at Google Ad Settings.

7. Impact of Blocking Cookies

If you choose to block or delete cookies, some parts of our website may not function properly:

  • Blocking essential cookies: Core website features such as form submissions, session persistence, and security protections may not work. You may be unable to submit contact or demo request forms.
  • Blocking analytics cookies: We will not be able to understand how you use our website, which limits our ability to improve the user experience. This does not affect your ability to use the website.
  • Blocking marketing cookies: You may still see Darsync advertisements, but they will not be tailored to your interests or browsing history. We will not be able to accurately measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the cookies we use, technology, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you via a cookie banner or email.
