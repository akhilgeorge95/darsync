1. Uptime Commitment

Darsync commits to a 99.9% monthly uptime for the Darsync platform, measured as the percentage of total minutes in a calendar month during which the Service is available and operational.

This translates to a maximum of approximately 43 minutes of unplanned downtime per month.

Uptime is calculated as follows:

Uptime % = ((Total Minutes - Unplanned Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes) x 100

Planned maintenance windows (see Section 2) are excluded from the uptime calculation.

2. Planned Maintenance

To ensure the continued reliability and security of the Service, we perform regular planned maintenance during the following window:

Detail Specification
Maintenance Window Friday, 2:00 AM - 4:00 AM GST (Gulf Standard Time, UTC+4)
Frequency As needed, typically 1-2 times per month
Advance Notice Minimum 72 hours via email and in-app notification
Impact Service may be partially or fully unavailable during this window

Friday was selected as the maintenance window because it is the weekend in the UAE/GCC region, minimizing impact on business operations. Emergency maintenance outside this window will be communicated with as much advance notice as possible.

3. Incident Response Times

We classify incidents by severity and commit to the following response times:

Severity Definition Response Time Resolution Target
Critical (P1) Service is completely unavailable for all users; data loss or security breach 1 hour 4 hours
High (P2) Major feature is non-functional; significant performance degradation affecting multiple users 4 hours 8 hours
Medium (P3) Feature is impaired but workaround available; performance degradation affecting limited users 8 hours 24 hours
Low (P4) Minor issue; cosmetic defect; general question or feature request 24 hours Next release cycle

Response time is defined as the time between when an incident is reported (or detected by our monitoring systems) and when a Darsync engineer begins actively working on the issue.

Resolution target is the target time to restore the Service to normal operation. Resolution targets are best-effort goals and not guaranteed commitments.

Enterprise customers may have access to additional support channels, including a dedicated account manager and priority phone line, as specified in their individual service agreement.

5. Escalation Process

If you feel that an issue is not being resolved in a timely manner, you may escalate through the following process:

  1. Level 1 - Support Team: Initial point of contact. All issues start here. Contact via email, phone, or live chat.
  2. Level 2 - Support Manager: If the issue is not resolved within the response time or you are unsatisfied with the resolution, request escalation to the Support Manager via email at support-manager@darsync.ae.
  3. Level 3 - Head of Engineering: For critical issues (P1/P2) that remain unresolved beyond the resolution target, escalate to the Head of Engineering via email at engineering@darsync.ae.
  4. Level 4 - Executive Management: For issues that remain unresolved after Level 3 escalation, or for matters requiring executive attention, contact exec@darsync.ae.

At each escalation level, you will receive acknowledgment within the response time specified for the issue severity, and regular updates until the issue is resolved.

6. Service Credits

If Darsync fails to meet the 99.9% uptime commitment in any calendar month, you are entitled to service credits as follows:

Credit calculation: Service credits are calculated at 10x the downtime. For every minute of unplanned downtime beyond the 99.9% threshold, you receive a credit equivalent to 10 minutes of your subscription cost.

Monthly Uptime Service Credit
99.0% - 99.89% 10x downtime credit (applied to next invoice)
95.0% - 98.99% 10x downtime credit + 10% of monthly fee
Below 95.0% 10x downtime credit + 25% of monthly fee

How to claim: To claim service credits, submit a request to support@darsync.ae within 30 days of the month in which the downtime occurred. Include the dates and times of the downtime experienced. Credits will be verified against our monitoring data and applied to your next invoice.

Limitations: Service credits are the sole and exclusive remedy for downtime. Credits cannot exceed 100% of the monthly subscription fee for the affected month and are not redeemable for cash.

7. Exclusions

The uptime commitment and service credits do not apply to downtime caused by:

  • Planned maintenance during the designated maintenance window
  • Force majeure events (natural disasters, acts of war, government actions, pandemic)
  • Third-party service or infrastructure failures outside our control (e.g., cloud provider outages, DNS issues, internet backbone disruptions)
  • Customer actions, including misconfiguration, excessive API usage, or violation of the Acceptable Use Policy
  • Browser or client-side issues
  • Features or services explicitly designated as "Beta" or "Preview"

8. Reporting & Transparency

Darsync is committed to transparency regarding service availability:

  • Status Page: Real-time service status is available at status.darsync.ae, including current and historical uptime data.
  • Incident Reports: For Critical and High severity incidents, we publish a post-incident report within 5 business days detailing the root cause, impact, resolution, and preventive measures.
  • Monthly Reports: Enterprise customers receive a monthly uptime and performance report as part of their service agreement.
