1. Definitions & Scope

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between Darsync Technologies LLC ("Processor," "we," or "Darsync") and the Customer ("Controller" or "you") for the provision of Darsync property management services (the "Service").

This DPA applies to all personal data processed by Darsync on behalf of the Controller in connection with the Service. The terms "personal data," "processing," "data subject," "data controller," "data processor," and "supervisory authority" shall have the meanings given to them in the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and, where applicable, equivalent terms under the UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data.

2. Processor & Controller Roles

2.1 The Controller

The Customer is the data controller with respect to all personal data that the Customer inputs, uploads, or processes through the Service ("Customer Data"). The Controller determines the purposes and means of processing Customer Data.

2.2 The Processor

Darsync is the data processor with respect to Customer Data. Darsync processes Customer Data solely on the documented instructions of the Controller and only for the purposes of providing the Service.

2.3 Obligations of the Processor

Darsync shall:

  • Process Customer Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required by applicable law (in which case Darsync will inform the Controller before processing, unless prohibited by law)
  • Ensure that persons authorized to process Customer Data are bound by confidentiality obligations
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the security of processing
  • Assist the Controller in responding to data subject requests and fulfilling data protection impact assessment obligations
  • Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA
  • Immediately inform the Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes applicable data protection law

The Controller grants Darsync general written authorization to engage sub-processors for the processing of Customer Data. Darsync maintains a list of current sub-processors and will notify the Controller at least 30 days before adding or replacing a sub-processor.

4.2 Objection to Sub-Processors

If the Controller has a legitimate objection to a new sub-processor, the Controller must notify Darsync in writing within 14 days of receiving notice. The parties will work in good faith to resolve the objection. If no resolution is reached, the Controller may terminate the affected Service component without penalty.

5. International Data Transfers

Where Customer Data is transferred outside the country in which it was collected, Darsync ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): For transfers of data originating in the EEA/UK to countries without an adequate level of data protection, Darsync enters into the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914) with the relevant sub-processors.
  • Transfer Impact Assessments: Darsync conducts transfer impact assessments for each international data transfer to evaluate the level of data protection in the destination country and implement supplementary measures where necessary.
  • Data Localization Options: Enterprise customers may request data residency in specific regions (UAE or EU). Data localization options are available upon request and may be subject to additional fees.

6. Security Measures

Darsync implements and maintains the following technical and organizational security measures:

6.1 Encryption

  • Data in Transit: All data transmitted between your devices and Darsync servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Data at Rest: All Customer Data stored on our servers is encrypted using AES-256 encryption.
  • Key Management: Encryption keys are managed through a dedicated key management service with automatic key rotation.

6.2 Access Controls

  • Authentication: Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is enforced for all internal access to production systems.
  • Authorization: Role-based access control (RBAC) ensures that personnel only access data necessary for their job function.
  • Access Logging: All access to Customer Data is logged and auditable, with logs retained for a minimum of 12 months.
  • Periodic Reviews: Access rights are reviewed quarterly and revoked immediately upon role change or termination.

6.3 Infrastructure Security

  • SOC 2 Type II certified cloud infrastructure
  • Network segmentation and firewalls
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS)
  • Regular vulnerability scanning and annual penetration testing
  • DDoS protection and web application firewall (WAF)

6.4 Organizational Measures

  • Background checks for all employees with access to Customer Data
  • Annual security awareness training
  • Documented security policies and procedures
  • Dedicated security team responsible for monitoring and incident response
  • Business continuity and disaster recovery plans tested annually

7. Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach affecting Customer Data, Darsync will:

  1. Notify the Controller without undue delay and within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach. Notification will include:
    • Description of the nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects and personal data records affected
    • Contact details of Darsync's Data Protection Officer
    • Description of the likely consequences of the breach
    • Description of the measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including measures to mitigate its effects
  2. Investigate and remediate the breach promptly, taking all necessary steps to contain the breach and prevent further unauthorized access.
  3. Cooperate with the Controller in the Controller's efforts to notify affected data subjects and/or supervisory authorities as required by applicable law.
  4. Provide follow-up reports detailing the root cause of the breach, full impact assessment, and preventive measures implemented.

8. Audit Rights

The Controller has the right to verify Darsync's compliance with this DPA through audits:

  • Documentation Audit: Darsync will make available, upon request, all relevant documentation demonstrating compliance with this DPA, including security certifications, penetration test summaries, and SOC 2 reports.
  • On-Site Audit: The Controller may conduct on-site audits of Darsync's data processing facilities, subject to the following conditions:
    • 30 days' advance written notice
    • Audits conducted during normal business hours
    • The auditor must sign a non-disclosure agreement
    • The audit must not unreasonably interfere with Darsync's operations
    • Maximum of one audit per calendar year (unless a data breach has occurred)
  • Third-Party Audit: The Controller may appoint a qualified, independent third-party auditor to conduct the audit on its behalf, subject to the same conditions above.

Darsync will cooperate fully with any audit and provide reasonable assistance. The costs of audits are borne by the Controller, unless the audit reveals material non-compliance by Darsync.

9. Data Subject Rights

Darsync will assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection) by:

  • Promptly forwarding any data subject requests received directly by Darsync to the Controller
  • Providing the Controller with the technical capability to respond to data subject requests through the Service's administrative interface
  • Providing additional assistance upon request, which may be subject to a reasonable fee

10. Data Deletion & Return

Upon termination of the Service agreement:

  • Darsync will provide a 30-day window for the Controller to export Customer Data in standard formats (CSV, JSON, PDF).
  • After the export window, Darsync will delete all Customer Data from active systems within 30 days.
  • Customer Data in backup systems will be purged within 90 days of termination.
  • Darsync will provide written confirmation of data deletion upon request.

Darsync may retain anonymized or aggregated data that does not identify individual data subjects for analytics and service improvement purposes.

11. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set out in the main service agreement (Terms of Service). Nothing in this DPA limits either party's liability for breaches of its obligations under applicable data protection law to the extent that such liability cannot be limited under that law.
