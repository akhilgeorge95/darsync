Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Darsync pricing and billing.

Every Darsync plan comes with a 14-day free trial that includes full access to all features in your selected tier. No credit card is required to start. You can add your properties, invite team members, and explore every feature before committing to a paid plan.

You pay a flat monthly fee for each user who has access to Darsync. There are no additional per-unit or per-property charges. Whether you manage 10 units or 10,000 units, the price stays the same per user. This makes budgeting simple and predictable as your portfolio grows.

Yes, you can upgrade or downgrade your plan at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately and you will be charged a prorated amount for the remainder of your billing cycle. Downgrades take effect at the start of your next billing cycle, so you will not lose access to any features mid-period.

We accept all major credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express), bank transfers, and wire transfers. For Enterprise plans, we also support purchase orders and custom invoicing terms. All transactions are processed securely through PCI-compliant payment processors.

No, there are no setup fees for Starter or Professional plans. You can be up and running within minutes. Enterprise plans include complimentary onboarding, data migration assistance, and dedicated implementation support at no additional cost.

Yes. Switching to annual billing saves you up to 17% compared to monthly billing. The Starter plan drops from $49 to $41/user/month, Professional from $100 to $85/user/month, and Enterprise from $150 to $127/user/month. You can toggle between monthly and annual pricing at the top of this page to see the difference.

When your 14-day trial ends, you can choose a paid plan to continue using Darsync with all your data intact. Your data and configurations are preserved for 30 days after trial expiry, so there is no pressure. We will send you reminder emails before your trial ends so you have plenty of time to decide.
