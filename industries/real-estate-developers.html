The Challenges

Why Developer-to-Operations Transitions Fail

The transition from construction to property management is one of the most critical and often mismanaged phases in real estate development.

🔑

Handover Management

Handing over hundreds of units simultaneously involves inspections, document transfers, key handovers, utility activations, and owner onboarding. Without structured workflows, critical steps are missed, delays compound, and buyer satisfaction plummets.

🔎

Snagging & Deficiency Lists

Post-construction defects must be documented, categorized, assigned to contractors, tracked to resolution, and verified. Paper-based snagging processes lose items, create disputes, and slow resolution to months instead of weeks.

📋

Warranty Tracking

Building components have different warranty periods from different contractors. Tracking warranty expiry dates, filing claims before deadlines, and documenting defects requires systematic management that spreadsheets cannot provide.

🔄

Development-to-FM Transition

The handoff from development teams to facilities management is often chaotic. Asset registers, equipment manuals, vendor contracts, and operational procedures must be transferred completely. Gaps in this transition create long-term operational problems.
