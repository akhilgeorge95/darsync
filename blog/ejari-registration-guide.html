Complete Guide to Ejari Registration in Dubai — 2026 Update 📜

April 5, 2026 · 9 min read

Ejari is the mandatory tenancy contract registration system in Dubai, administered by the Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA) under the Dubai Land Department (DLD). Every rental agreement in Dubai — whether residential or commercial — must be registered through Ejari to be legally enforceable. For property managers handling dozens or hundreds of leases, Ejari registration is a critical compliance requirement that demands accuracy, timeliness, and careful documentation.

This guide covers everything you need to know about Ejari registration in Dubai for 2026, including the step-by-step process, required documents, current fees, common mistakes that cause delays, and how modern property management software can automate the entire workflow.

📚 What Is Ejari and Why Does It Matter?

Ejari (which means "my rent" in Arabic) was launched by the Dubai government to regulate the rental market and protect both landlords and tenants. The system creates a transparent record of all tenancy agreements, which serves multiple purposes:

  • Legal protection: An Ejari-registered contract is the only form of tenancy agreement recognized by Dubai courts. Without registration, neither the landlord nor the tenant can enforce the terms of their lease in a legal dispute.
  • DEWA connection: Tenants need an active Ejari certificate to set up water and electricity services through DEWA (Dubai Electricity and Water Authority).
  • Visa processing: Expatriate tenants require an Ejari certificate to process or renew their residence visa, making it a critical document for their legal status in the UAE.
  • Rental dispute resolution: The Rental Dispute Settlement Centre (RDSC) only accepts cases where the tenancy agreement is Ejari-registered.
  • Market transparency: Ejari data feeds into the RERA rental index, which determines permissible rent increases and helps regulate the market.

For property managers, failing to register leases with Ejari can result in fines, tenant complaints, inability to process visa applications, and legal exposure in case of disputes. It is not optional — it is a fundamental compliance requirement.

📄 Required Documents for Ejari Registration

Before beginning the registration process, gather the following documents. Missing or incorrect documents are the most common cause of registration delays.

For the Landlord / Property Manager:

  • Title deed of the property (copy)
  • Landlord's Emirates ID or passport (copy)
  • Power of Attorney (POA) if the property manager is registering on behalf of the landlord
  • Trade license if the landlord is a company
  • RERA registration certificate (for property management companies)

For the Tenant:

  • Emirates ID (copy, front and back)
  • Passport with valid visa page (copy)
  • For company tenants: trade license and authorized signatory's Emirates ID

Property Documents:

  • Signed tenancy contract (original)
  • Previous Ejari certificate (for renewals and modifications)
  • DEWA premise number

🛠 Step-by-Step Ejari Registration Process

Step 1: Access the Ejari Portal

Registration can be completed through the official Dubai REST (Real Estate Self Transaction) app or through authorized typing centers across Dubai. Property management companies with RERA registration can access the portal directly for bulk registrations.

Step 2: Create or Log In to Your Account

If this is your first time using the system, you will need to create an account using your Emirates ID. For property management companies, your company account should already be set up with your RERA registration linked.

Step 3: Select the Registration Type

Choose from new registration, renewal, cancellation, or amendment. Each type has slightly different requirements and fees. Ensure you select the correct type to avoid rejection.

Step 4: Enter Contract Details

Input all tenancy contract details including the property address, contract dates, annual rent amount, number of cheques, security deposit amount, and any special conditions. Accuracy here is critical — discrepancies between the submitted information and the actual contract will cause rejection.

Step 5: Upload Required Documents

Upload scanned copies of all the documents listed above. Ensure they are clear, complete, and current. Expired documents will be rejected.

Step 6: Pay the Registration Fee

As of 2026, Ejari registration fees are as follows:

  • New registration: AED 220 (including knowledge fee and innovation fee)
  • Renewal: AED 220
  • Cancellation: AED 110
  • Amendment: AED 110

Payment can be made via credit/debit card, ePay, or at authorized typing centers. Note that typing centers may charge an additional service fee of AED 50-100.

Step 7: Receive the Ejari Certificate

Once the registration is approved (typically within 24-48 hours for online submissions, same day at typing centers), you will receive the Ejari certificate with a unique Ejari number. This certificate should be stored securely and provided to the tenant for their records.

⚠ Common Mistakes That Cause Delays

After processing thousands of Ejari registrations, we have identified the most common mistakes that cause rejections and delays:

  1. Mismatched rent amounts: The rent amount entered in Ejari must exactly match the total annual rent in the signed contract. Even small discrepancies (like rounding errors) will cause rejection.
  2. Expired Emirates IDs or visas: The system validates document expiry dates. If either the landlord's or tenant's Emirates ID is expired or expiring within the contract period, registration will be rejected.
  3. Incorrect property details: The property address and unit number in Ejari must match the title deed exactly. Common errors include using informal building names instead of official names, or incorrect unit numbering.
  4. Missing POA for agents: If you are registering on behalf of a landlord, you must have a valid, notarized Power of Attorney that specifically authorizes you to manage Ejari registrations. Generic POAs may be rejected.
  5. Late registration: While there is no official penalty for late registration, RERA expects registration within 30 days of contract signing. Significant delays may draw regulatory attention.
  6. Forgetting to cancel previous registrations: Before registering a new lease for the same unit, the previous Ejari registration must be cancelled. Overlapping registrations for the same unit will be rejected.

🔗 Ejari and Related Compliance Requirements

Ejari registration does not exist in isolation. It connects to several other compliance requirements that property managers must track:

  • RERA rental index compliance: Ejari data is used to determine whether proposed rent increases comply with the RERA rental index calculator. Non-compliant increases can be challenged by tenants.
  • Service charge audits: RERA requires annual service charge audits for managed properties, and Ejari registration is a prerequisite for certain filings.
  • DLD annual reporting: The Dubai Land Department requires property management companies to submit annual reports that reference Ejari registration data.
