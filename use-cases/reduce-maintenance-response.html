Stop Losing Tenants to Slow Repairs. Respond in Minutes, Not Days.

Replace phone-tag and paper notes with automated ticket routing, real-time SLA tracking, and instant tenant updates. Cut maintenance response time by 75%.

The Problem

Slow Maintenance Response Is the #1 Reason Tenants Don't Renew

When a tenant's AC breaks in 45-degree heat, every minute counts. But in most property management companies, a simple repair request passes through 4-5 people before anyone picks up a wrench. By the time the technician arrives, the tenant is already browsing other listings.

❌ The Manual Way

8 Steps of Phone Tag

  1. Tenant calls the office -- Tenant phones the reception desk, often getting voicemail or a busy signal during peak hours.
  2. Receptionist writes a note -- The request is scribbled on paper or typed into a spreadsheet with limited details.
  3. Manager reads the note -- Hours later, the property manager reviews the note and tries to understand the issue.
  4. Assigns a technician -- Manager calls or messages available technicians, often without knowing who is nearest or free.
  5. Technician calls the tenant -- The technician phones the tenant to understand the problem and schedule a visit.
  6. Schedules the visit -- Back-and-forth to find a time that works, often delayed by a day or more.
  7. Performs the repair -- Technician visits, diagnoses, and fixes -- sometimes needing parts that require a second visit.
  8. Updates the manager -- Technician calls or texts the manager that the job is done. Manager updates the spreadsheet.

Average response time: 2-3 hours to first contact, 1-2 days to resolution

✅ The Darsync Way

3 Steps. Real-Time Tracking.

  1. Tenant submits via portal or WhatsApp -- Tenants log requests through the self-service portal or simply send a WhatsApp message. Photos, location, and urgency are captured automatically.
  2. Auto-assign nearest available technician -- Darsync's smart routing engine assigns the request to the nearest qualified technician based on skill set, location, and current workload. The technician receives instant mobile notification.
  3. SLA tracks completion in real-time -- Every request is tracked against configurable SLA targets. Tenants receive real-time status updates. Managers see live dashboards with escalation alerts for at-risk tickets.

Average response time: 30-60 minutes to first contact, same-day resolution
